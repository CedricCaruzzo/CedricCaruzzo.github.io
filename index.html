About

I am an AI researcher working at the intersection of computer vision, self-supervised learning, and foundation models. My research focuses on developing robust, generalizable AI systems for digital pathology and oncology, with the ultimate goal of creating clinically impactful applications that can improve cancer diagnosis and treatment.

Currently at Lunit, I develop large-scale vision models trained on histopathology data, multimodal systems that integrate various data modalities, and work with GPU clusters for training foundation models. My work spans from fundamental research in self-supervised learning to applied AI for biomarker discovery and clinical decision support.

Research Interests

My research focuses on developing AI systems that can understand and reason about biological and medical imagery at scale, with particular emphasis on foundation models, multimodal learning, and their applications in oncology and drug discovery.

Current Focus Areas

Foundation Models for Digital Pathology: Training large-scale vision transformers on gigapixel histopathology images to learn generalizable representations for downstream cancer research tasks.

Multimodal AI in Oncology: Integrating histopathology with other data modalities (genomics, radiology, clinical data) to provide comprehensive views of cancer biology for improved diagnosis and treatment planning.

Self-Supervised Learning: Developing methods that learn meaningful representations from unlabeled data, addressing the challenge of limited annotated medical imaging datasets.
